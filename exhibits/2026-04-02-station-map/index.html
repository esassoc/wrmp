Reading the Map

The WRMP's fish and fish habitat monitoring program covers the full salinity gradient of the San Francisco Estuary — from the freshwater-influenced Suisun Marsh in the east, through the brackish waters of San Pablo Bay, to the more saline South Bay. This gradient is the single strongest driver of which species live where.

Why station placement matters: The monitoring network includes benchmark sites (long-term reference), project sites (active restoration), and planned restoration sites. By sampling across all three, scientists can directly compare: are restored wetlands supporting the same fish communities as healthy natural marshes?

Switch the color mode above to explore different dimensions of the network. Region shows the three Bay zones. Network reveals the nine sampling circuits that field crews follow. Habitat distinguishes sloughs, open bay, and tidal ponds. Site Type shows the restoration context of each station.

What's Next

When catch data arrives from UC Davis, each station marker will grow to reflect the abundance and diversity of fish captured there. The map will become a living portrait of the estuary's ecological health — showing not just where we look, but what we find.
