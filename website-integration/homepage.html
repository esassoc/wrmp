Where are our wetlands?

Tidal wetlands ringed most of the Bay prior to the mid-1800s. By 1998, more than 80% of wetlands had been lost to development. Today the WRMP tracks progress towards protecting and restoring remaining wetlands around the Bay.

Baylands Geography Wetlands & Sea Level Rise

How healthy are our wetlands?

Wetlands with clean water and healthy habitats offer more benefits to people and wildlife and are resilient to environmental hazards.

Wetland Condition Water Quality

Who uses our wetlands?

Tidal wetlands provide habitat for fish, wildlife, and plants, recreational opportunities for people, and environmental benefits to nearby communities.

Our mission

The WRMP delivers coordinated regional monitoring of the San Francisco Estuary's wetlands to: Inform science-based decision-making for wetland restoration and adaptive management, and Increase the cost-effectiveness of permit-driven monitoring associated with wetland restoration projects.

Learn more

Our commitment to justice and equity

The WRMP is committed to equity and environmental justice by ensuring that wetland restoration benefits all communities, particularly those most vulnerable to climate change. We prioritize inclusive collaboration, equitable access to data, and policies that enhance resilience, public health, and cultural connections to the San Francisco Estuary's tidal wetlands.
