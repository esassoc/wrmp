Tagging & recognizing exhibits

The Science Framework's question color system, and the exhibit-poster decisions that build on it — the poster badges inherit the same per-question hues. Approve the color system, then one option in each poster section.

Science Framework question colors

Each guiding question gets a WRMP-brand hue with a light-to-dark ramp across the three hierarchy levels — guiding question, management question, priority recommended action — plus the exhibit badge, which draws from the same ramp. The five hues run cool-to-warm as a natural landscape ramp — sky blue, grass, sand, orange, earth. Every tier meets WCAG AA contrast.

In context

The three warm hues — Sand, Orange, Earth — consecutive in the Science Framework layout, so light-gold vs burnt-orange vs deep-brown reads in real content.

Guiding Question 3

What new information do we need to better understand regional lessons from tidal wetland restoration projects?

Management Question 3A 3A

What new information do we need to better understand regional lessons from tidal marsh restoration projects and ensure their continued success?

Management Question 3B 3B

Where and when can interventions — dredged-sediment placement, watershed reconnection, living shorelines — help sustain tidal marsh ecosystems?

Priority Recommended Action

Pilot sediment-placement and living-shoreline projects at index sites, and track marsh response against untreated references.

Guiding Question 4

How do policies, programs, and projects to protect and restore tidal marshes affect the distribution, abundance, and health of plants and animals?

Management Question 4A 4A

How are habitats for assemblages of resident species of fish and wildlife in tidal marsh ecosystems changing over time?

Management Question 4B 4B

How are the distribution and abundance of key resident species of fish and wildlife of tidal marsh ecosystems changing over time?

Priority Recommended Action

Establish index stations for resident fish and wildlife assemblages and track community change alongside restoration milestones.

Guiding Question 5

How do efforts to protect and restore tidal wetlands benefit and/or impact public health, safety, and recreation?

Management Question 5A 5A

What mosquito and vector control strategies need to be considered in restoration design and management?

Management Question 5C 5C

How are the benefits of wetlands — flood-risk reduction, water quality, public access — distributed regionally?

Priority Recommended Action

Coordinate vector control and public-access planning into restoration design, and track wetland benefits across communities over time.

C. Topic indicator on the cover

Exhibits also belong to site topics (Fish & Wildlife, Baylands Geography, …). The topic is a quieter, neutral signal — deliberately not guiding-question-colored — so the two systems never get confused. The colored management-question badge stays the bright overlay; topic reads as gray metadata.

C1 — Neutral topic chip on the cover

A small neutral chip with a leading map icon, set just above the title — clear of the top-left badge and top-right marker. Tradeoff: topic is visible on the poster itself, at the cost of one more element on the image.
